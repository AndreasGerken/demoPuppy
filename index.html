    The demoPuppy Robot

    A puppy robot created (sometime in 2010) for demos (and some research) at the AI Lab, University of Zurich. The robot is designed to feature interchangeable legs, actuators and spine and to be used with an Arduino Uno micro-controller.

    The body has been designed with input from Juan Pablo Carbajal. The legs are a modified - 3D-printer optimized - version of a CNC-milled design from Emanuel Benker.

    It comes in two configurations, a rigid and a flexible spine assembly. The flexible spine assembly is to be used with a 3mm diameter bungee elastic cord and a cam cleat (source files included). The spine stiffness can be varied by adjusting the bungee cord tension. Torsion springs are used to keep the cleat in place.
